How to Read the Oahu Snorkel Score

The snorkel score is meant to be a fast, practical summary of several ocean conditions at once. It works best when you read the number together with the written reasons behind it.

What the number means

A higher score usually means several conditions are lining up well at the same time: calmer water, lower chop, more manageable current, and better visibility-related weather. A lower score usually means one or more of those pieces are working against you.

Why the written reasons matter

Two spots can have the same score for different reasons. One region might be held back mostly by waves, while another might be calmer but have strong chop or poor visibility conditions. The written notes tell you what is actually driving the number.

Use the score as a filter, not a guarantee

The score is there to help narrow the field quickly, not to replace local judgment. It is best used as a strong starting point, especially when you compare multiple Oahu shore regions side by side and look at how the score changes through the day.
