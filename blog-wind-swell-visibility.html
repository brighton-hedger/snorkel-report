Wind, Swell, and Visibility Are Not the Same

One of the easiest mistakes in snorkeling forecasts is treating every ocean number as the same kind of signal. They are not. Swell, wind chop, and visibility-related weather can all affect the experience differently.

Swell tells you one part of the story

Swell can bring open-ocean energy into a shoreline, but it does not always describe how messy the surface feels in the water. A moderate swell with some shelter can still feel better than a lower swell with more local chop.

Wind chop changes surface texture fast

Wind-driven surface chop can make the water feel rougher, reduce comfort, and make it harder to see clearly into the water even if the underlying swell does not look extreme on paper.

Visibility has its own weather story

Cloud cover and rain do not just affect the sky. They can change underwater light, make the water feel flatter visually, and sometimes line up with runoff or murkier-looking conditions. That is why the site looks at weather alongside marine conditions.
